I am an interdisciplinary health and environmental scientist. My research examines the social and environmental determinants of infectious disease. Most recently, I have studied the human-environment dynamics of a snail-borne parasitic disease (schistosomiasis) in a dammed landscapes in West Africa.

I recently finished my PhD in the Emmett Interdisciplinary Program in Environment and Resources (E-IPER) at Stanford University. I also hold an MPH in global epidemiology from Emory University's Rollins School of Public Health and a BA in biology and Spanish from the University of Minnesota, Morris . Read more about me.
